VA Loans

VA Loan Guide for California Veterans & Active Military — 2026

SoCal Home Network • Updated June 2026 • 7 min read

If you've served in the U.S. military, the VA home loan benefit is one of the most powerful tools available to you — and one of the most underused. VA loans offer zero down payment, no private mortgage insurance, and competitive interest rates that are often lower than conventional loans. For Southern California veterans, this benefit can be the difference between renting indefinitely and owning a home.

The Core VA Loan Benefits

No Down Payment

Purchase a home with 0% down — even in high-cost SoCal markets. No down payment required for eligible borrowers using their full entitlement.

No PMI

VA loans never require private mortgage insurance, saving eligible borrowers hundreds of dollars per month compared to FHA or low-down conventional loans.

Competitive Rates

VA loans consistently offer some of the lowest interest rates available because the VA guarantee reduces risk for lenders.

Flexible Credit

No official minimum credit score from the VA — most lenders approve VA loans with a 580-620+ score, and some work with lower scores.

Limits on Closing Costs

The VA limits certain fees that lenders can charge, reducing your out-of-pocket costs at closing.

Assumable Loans

VA loans are assumable — a future buyer can take over your loan at your original rate, which is a powerful selling tool in a rising-rate environment.

Who Is Eligible for a VA Loan?

VA loan eligibility is based on your military service history. You may be eligible if you are:

  • Active duty: Served at least 90 continuous days
  • Veteran: Served 181 days during peacetime or 90 days during wartime, with other-than-dishonorable discharge
  • National Guard or Reserve: Served at least 6 years, or were activated to federal active duty
  • Surviving spouse: Of a veteran who died in service or from a service-connected disability, and have not remarried

To confirm eligibility, you'll need a Certificate of Eligibility (COE). Your lender can typically obtain this for you electronically through the VA's system — it takes minutes in most cases.

VA Loan Limits in California in 2026

Since January 2020, veterans with full entitlement have no VA loan limit — you can borrow as much as a lender will approve with zero down payment. This is a major advantage in high-cost SoCal markets.

VA "conforming loan limits" still matter if you have reduced entitlement (for example, if you have an existing VA loan). In that case, the 2026 conforming limit in high-cost counties like LA and Orange is $1,209,750 for a single-family home.

The VA Funding Fee

The main cost unique to VA loans is the VA funding fee — a one-time charge that helps fund the VA loan program. The fee for 2026:

  • First use, 0% down: 2.15% of the loan amount
  • First use, 5-9.99% down: 1.5%
  • First use, 10%+ down: 1.25%
  • Subsequent use: 3.3% (0% down)
  • Exempt: Veterans receiving VA disability compensation are exempt from the funding fee entirely

The funding fee can be rolled into the loan amount, so you don't need cash at closing to cover it. On a $600,000 loan with first-time use and 0% down, the fee is $12,900 — still significantly less than what you'd spend on a down payment or PMI over time.

VA Loans in the SoCal Market: What to Know

Seller Acceptance

Some sellers in competitive SoCal markets have historically been hesitant to accept VA offers due to misconceptions about VA appraisals and closing timelines. The reality is that VA loans close reliably and the appraisal process is similar to conventional. Working with an experienced VA buyer's agent who knows how to present VA offers effectively is important.

VA Appraisal Requirements

VA appraisers ensure the home meets Minimum Property Requirements (MPRs) — essentially that the home is safe, structurally sound, and sanitary. Major issues like roof damage, electrical problems, or pest damage will be flagged. In most cases, these are negotiated with the seller to repair before closing.

Great Military Markets in SoCal

Southern California is home to significant military installations — Camp Pendleton (Oceanside), Naval Base San Diego, Edwards Air Force Base, March Air Reserve Base, Naval Air Weapons Station China Lake, and more. VA loans are extremely active in communities near these bases, and local lenders and agents are very familiar with the VA process.

How to Use Your VA Loan Benefit: Step by Step

  1. Confirm your eligibility — Know your service history and get your COE
  2. Find a VA-experienced lender — Not all lenders are equally experienced with VA loans; find one who does them regularly
  3. Get pre-approved — Understand your buying power before you start shopping
  4. Work with a VA-knowledgeable buyer's agent — They'll know how to structure your offer and navigate the VA appraisal process
  5. Make your offer — Your agent can help position your VA offer competitively
  6. VA appraisal and underwriting — Typically 30-45 days from accepted offer to close
